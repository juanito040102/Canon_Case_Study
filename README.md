# Canon Case Study – Digital Analytics Dashboard

**Keyword Research & Performance Dashboard** | *Juan Zumarán*

---

## The Problem

Canon was losing organic visibility to Sony on key searches like *"best camera for content creators"* — despite being a market leader. Paid traffic tracking was broken, and content didn't match user intent.

**Goal:** Identify where Canon can win back organic search traffic for beginner photographer and content creator keywords.

---

## My Role

Led **keyword research** using Semrush + Google Sheets:
- Analyzed 847 keywords (Canon vs. Sony)
- Segmented by intent, topic, and product
- Built performance dashboards
- Recommended content strategy

---

## Key Insights

| Insight | Finding |
|---------|---------|
| Volume imbalance | High-volume keywords owned by competitors |
| Easy wins exist | Beginner/vlogging keywords are under-targeted |
| Traffic mix | 80% paid / 20% organic — paid masking organic issues |
| Intent mismatch | 29% of "informational" keywords were actually navigational/commercial |

---

## Opportunities vs. Threats

**Opportunities** (Canon avg position #12, KD 48%):
- ~90K monthly searches for video/vlog cameras
- 2K monthly searches for beginner terms

**Threats** (Canon avg position #43, KD 54%):
- ~70K monthly searches for video/vlog cameras
- 15K monthly searches for beginner terms

---

## Recommendations

| Priority | Action |
|----------|--------|
| Quick wins | Fix metadata, target low-difficulty keywords |
| Medium | Create top-of-funnel content (site + social) |
| Long-term | Target high-volume terms like *"vlog camera"* and *"best beginner camera"* |

---

## What I Learned

Even huge brands like Canon have basic, fixable SEO gaps. One semester project could realistically move the needle — if they run the same intent-mapping process for other product categories.
